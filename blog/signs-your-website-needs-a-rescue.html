5 Signs Your Website Needs a Rescue

by Good Bones Team July 12, 2026 Site Rescue Performance Small Business

Slow load times, broken forms, and mobile headaches are costing you customers. Here are five warning signs—and what to do about them.

Most small business owners know when something feels off about their website, but it is easy to ignore the symptoms until they start hurting sales. Here are five common signs your site needs a rescue.

1. It takes more than three seconds to load

Visitors expect speed. If your pages drag, people leave before they see your offer. A rescue usually starts with image optimization, code cleanup, and better hosting.

2. It looks broken on phones

More than half of local web traffic comes from mobile devices. If text is tiny, buttons are hard to tap, or layouts shift around, you are losing trust—and rankings.

3. Forms or links do not work

Broken contact forms, dead links, and checkout errors silently kill conversions. These should be found and fixed before they cost you another lead.

4. You cannot update it yourself

If changing a phone number means emailing a developer, your site is too fragile. A good rescue sets you up with a workflow you can actually manage.

5. It does not show up in local search

If customers in Edmonton cannot find you on Google, your site is not doing its job. Technical SEO, local schema, and clear content all help.
