Privacy Policy

Good Bones Web Inc. is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard personal information in plain language, and how you can exercise your rights under Canada's Bill C-36, the Protecting Privacy and Consumer Data Act.

Our commitment to your privacy

We appreciate you taking the time to read our Privacy Policy. Privacy is at the core of what we do at Good Bones Web Inc. — both in how we handle your information and in how we build websites for our clients. With Canada’s new Bill C-36, the Protecting Privacy and Consumer Data Act, privacy rules are evolving. We are updating our practices and our clients’ sites to stay ahead of these future responsibilities, so you can trust that your data — and your customers’ data — is in good hands.

Accountability

Good Bones Web Inc. is responsible for all personal information under our control, including information we entrust to service providers such as web hosts, email platforms, analytics tools, and payment processors. We have a privacy management program that covers staff training, complaint handling, access and correction requests, and public transparency about our practices.

What we collect and why

We only collect personal information that is necessary for specific, recorded purposes. The purposes and the types of information we collect are:

  • Responding to inquiries — name, email address, phone number, and business information you provide through our contact form or email.
  • Planning and delivering projects — details about your business, goals, timeline, and website needs.
  • Improving our website — anonymous usage data such as pages viewed and time spent, collected through privacy-focused analytics.

We do not collect sensitive personal information such as financial details, health information, or government IDs through this website unless a specific project clearly requires it and you provide express consent.

Consent

We ask for your express consent before collecting, using, or disclosing your personal information, unless another legal exception applies. When we request consent, we tell you the specific purpose, the type of information, any reasonably foreseeable consequences, and any third parties who may receive it. We use plain language and avoid jargon.

You can withdraw your consent at any time by contacting us. We will explain any consequences and stop collecting, using, or disclosing the affected information as soon as feasible. We will never require you to consent to unrelated data uses as a condition of working with us.

Use and disclosure limits

We use and disclose personal information only for the purposes we told you about, or for a new purpose only if we record that purpose and obtain consent when required. We limit collection and use to what a reasonable person would consider appropriate, weighing the sensitivity of the information, the business need, and whether less intrusive options are available.

We do not sell personal information. We may disclose information to trusted service providers who help us deliver our services, and only when they have agreed to appropriate privacy and security protections.

Service providers and cross-border transfers

Some of our service providers are located outside Canada or process data across provincial borders. Before any cross-border transfer, we assess privacy risks and put in place safeguards such as contractual protections or approved certification programs. We will disclose in this policy whether personal information may be transferred outside Canada where privacy implications are reasonably foreseeable.

Current service categories that may handle data include hosting, email and communication, analytics, and payment processing. We require each service provider to notify us of any privacy breach involving our data.

Retention and disposal

We keep personal information only as long as necessary to fulfill the original purpose, respond to access requests, meet legal obligations, or satisfy reasonable contract terms. We consider the sensitivity of the data when setting retention periods.

When retention ends, we permanently and irreversibly dispose of the information by secure deletion or anonymization. You may ask us in writing to dispose of your personal information if it was collected unlawfully, you have withdrawn consent, or it is no longer needed to provide our services. We will comply unless legal obligations or other valid exceptions apply, in which case we will give you written reasons.

Security and breach reporting

We protect personal information with physical, organizational, and technological safeguards that are proportionate to the sensitivity, quantity, and format of the data. These safeguards guard against loss, theft, and unauthorized access, disclosure, copying, use, or modification.

If a breach occurs that creates a real risk of significant harm, we will report it to the Digital Safety and Data Protection Commission of Canada, notify affected individuals directly and conspicuously, and notify any other organizations or government bodies that could help reduce harm. All notifications happen as soon as feasible after the breach is confirmed. We keep records of every breach and produce them if requested.

Your rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access: Ask us whether we hold personal information about you, how we use it, whether it has been disclosed, and request a copy. We respond within 30 days, in plain language, and can provide an alternative format if needed.
  • Correction: Ask us to correct inaccurate, incomplete, or out-of-date information. If appropriate, we will send the correction to any third party that has access to the information.
  • Withdraw consent and disposal: Withdraw consent or request disposal as described above.
  • Complaint: Complain about our privacy practices. We will investigate, respond, and make necessary changes.

We will help you prepare your request if you need assistance.

Automated decisions

We do not currently use automated decision systems to make predictions, recommendations, or decisions about individuals that have legal or similarly significant effects. If we introduce such a system, this policy will be updated to explain the types of information used, their source, and the principal reasons or factors behind the decisions, and we will provide a way for you to request a human review.

Children's privacy

Our services are directed to adults and businesses, not children under 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we learn that we have collected a child's personal information, we will dispose of it promptly. We treat any children's personal information as sensitive by default.

De-identification and anonymization

De-identified information is still considered personal information under Bill C-36 and remains protected. Anonymized information has been irreversibly and permanently modified so that no reasonably foreseeable risk of identifying an individual remains, and is no longer subject to this policy.

If we de-identify data, we use measures proportionate to its sensitivity and purpose, and we do not use de-identified data to re-identify individuals except where permitted by law.

Updates to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or the law, including Canada’s Bill C-36. When we do, we will change the “Last updated” date at the top of this page and encourage you to review the policy.
